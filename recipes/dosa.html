Dosa

Description

Dosa is a thin, crispy, and savory South Indian pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal. It's commonly served with coconut chutney and sambar, and is a staple breakfast item throughout India.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice (preferably parboiled rice or idli rice)
  • 1/3 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional, for better fermentation)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Steps

  1. Soak the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind them into a smooth batter using a little water, then mix together.
  3. Ferment the batter overnight or for 8â€“12 hours in a warm place until it rises and turns slightly bubbly.
  4. Add salt and mix the fermented batter gently.
  5. Heat a pan, lightly grease it, and pour a ladleful of batter.
  6. Spread the batter in a circular motion to make a thin dosa.
  7. Drizzle oil/ghee around the edges and cook until golden and crisp.
  8. Fold and serve hot with chutney or sambar.
